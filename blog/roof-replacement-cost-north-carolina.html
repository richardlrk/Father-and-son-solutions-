Factors That Affect Pricing

  • Roof size and slope – Steeper, larger roofs require more labor and safety equipment
  • Number of roofing layers – Removing old shingles adds to labor costs
  • Roofing accessories – Skylights, chimneys, and vents increase complexity
  • Permit fees – Vary by municipality in North Carolina
  • Seasonal timing – Peak season (spring/summer) may have higher prices

Is It Repair or Replace?

Consider replacement if:

  • Your roof is over 20 years old
  • Damage affects more than 30% of the roof
  • You have recurring leaks despite repairs
  • Visible sagging or structural issues

Financing Options

Many roofing contractors offer financing plans. Some homeowners use home equity loans or insurance claims (for storm damage) to fund replacements.
